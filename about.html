Integration of Screens Into Shanghai's Urban Landscape

Background

The city of Shanghai has become infamous for its rapid development into a modern city, turning agriculture wastelands into a financial district within two decades. Shanghai’s obsession with media facade has created a unique urban landscape with entire buildings faces functioning as large screens. In understanding how the current urban landscape was developed and shaped can be beneficial in predicting Shanghai’s future urban landscape and if Shanghai is in danger of shifting into a ‘Generic City’. A generic city has no history or identity, it is completely free of context.

The Project

The purpose of this project is to showcase how Shanghai may look in the future in which screens have only continuously been integrated into the landscape. The photos were taken with 360 degree camera, in which hyper-reality images where then edited into the image. User's have the ability to interact with the 360 degree photos and explore the different aspects of the hyper-reality.

Intructions

User can click into the two location on the main page. Each location is different and shows how a hyper-reality would look in different locations in Shanghai. Each location will lead to a page that has an interactive 360 degree photo in which users can use the mouse to scroll around.
